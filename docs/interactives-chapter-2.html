Section 1.1 Chapter 2

Interactive Diagram.

You can use this interactive diagram to adjust the magnitude and direction of the vector using point at the tip, the point of application of the vector by sliding the tail along the line of action, and the direction of the vector by moving the direction of the line of action.

Interactive Diagram.

The interactive shows \(\vec{A}+ \vec{B}= \vec{R}.\)

You can adjust the magnitude and direction of vectors with the tips and their position along the line of action with the tails. When they arranged tip-to-tail, the resultant vector will appear. The vectors are represented as scalar components multiplied by unit vector \(\ihat\text{.}\)

This is just a graphic representation of tip-to-tail addition; \(2\ihat + 3 \ihat = 5 \ihat\) regardless of where \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) are located on the line of action.

Interactive Diagram.

Move the red point to move the vector in space. Click the red point to switch between \(x\)-\(y\) mode and \(z\) mode.

The vector can be visualized as the diagonal of a rectangular box with the \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) and \(z\) components as the side lengths.

Interactive Diagram.

Move the red point to move the vector in space. Click the red point to switch between \(x\)-\(y\) mode and \(z\) mode.

Note that the red, blue and green triangles are right triangles although it is not always easy to see this. The vector is the hypotenuse and the adjacent sides are the \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) and \(z\) rectangular components. The rectangular components and the direction angles are shown.

Interactive Diagram.

Use the red point to move the tip of the vector anywhere on the spherical surface. The rectangular and spherical coordinates are shown.

Interactive Diagram.

Use the red point to move the tip of the vector along the cylindrical surface. The rectangular and cylindrical coordinates are shown.

Interactive Diagram.

This interactive shows the graphical relations between the unit vector \(\hat{\vec{F}}\) and the unit circle. \(\ihat\) and \(\jhat\) are the standard unit vectors in the \(x\) and \(y\) directions.

Interactive Diagram.

This interactive shows the graphical expression and component values of force \(\vec{F}\) and its unit vector \(\hat{\textbf{F}}\text{.}\)

Interactive Diagram.

This interactive shows \(\vec{r}_{AB}\text{,}\) the displacement vector from \(\vec{A}\) to \(\vec{B}\) and the corresponding unit displacement vector \(\lambda_{AB}\text{.}\)

You may change \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) by moving the red dots. Click the dot to switch between \(x\)-\(y\) mode and \(z\) mode. Coordinates of \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) can also be entered into the table directly.

Interactive Diagram.

This diagram shows two forces added together using the triangle and parallelogram rules. Change the slider to see the different interpretations. Note that polar coordinates are a better fit for graphing magnitude and direction, while Cartesian coordinates work better for rectangular components.

Interactive Diagram.

In this interactive you can rotate the vector \(\vec{F}\) and also rotate the coordinate system and see the components of \(\vec{F}\) in the \(x\)-\(y\) and \(x'\)-\(y'\) directions.

Interactive Diagram.

This interactive represents vector addition \(\vec{R} = \vec{A} + \vec{B} + \vec{C}\text{.}\)

You can change the magnitude and direction of the three components, and view the resultant. You can also see the rectangular components of the three component vectors.

Interactive Diagram.

This interactive shows the vector sum of \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\text{.}\)

You may change \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) by moving the red dots. Click dot to switch between \(x\)-\(y\) mode and \(z\) mode. Coordinates of \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) can also be entered into the table.

Interactive Diagram.

This interactive demonstrates the relationship between vectors \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) and the projection of \(\vec{B}\) onto \(\vec{A}\text{.}\) The checkbox switches between showing \(\proj_\vec{A}\vec{B}\) and \(\proj_\vec{B}\vec{A}\text{.}\)

Interactive Diagram.

This interactive shows the vector projection of \(\vec{A}\) on \(\vec{B}\) or of \(\vec{B}\) on \(\vec{A}\text{.}\)

You may change \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) by moving the red dots. Click dot to switch between \(x\)-\(y\) mode and \(z\) mode. Coordinates of \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) can also be entered into the table.
