Section 3.4 Three Dimensional Vectors

While we often idealize our three-dimensional world into two dimensions, there are many times we need to include the full 3D geometry of a system in our computations. Hence, this section is devoted to 3D vector computations. The topics covered in detail in the 2D sections are extended in this section, while several new topics apply to 3D vectors specifically.

For nearly all 3D computations, you need the \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) and \(z\) components of each vector before proceeding with the computations. If you are given the components upfront, then you are set to move forward, but sometimes vectors are defined by either direction cosine angles or spherical angles. The next sections detail how you can move back and forth between these angular systems and \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) and \(z\) components.
