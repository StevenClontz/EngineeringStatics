Another simplification we will be making is to treat all forces as concentrated. Concentrated forces act at a single point, have a well defined line of action, and can be represented with an arrow — in other words, they are vectors. Real forces don’t actually act at a single mathematical point but concentrating them is intuitive and will be justified in a later chapter 7.8. You’re already familiar with the concept if you have ever placed all the weight of an object at its center of gravity.
