The dot product of two vectors \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) is defined as the sum of the product of the vector components in each coordinate direction. For three-dimensional vectors\(\vec{A} = \langle A_x, A_y, A_z \rangle\text{,}\) and \(\vec{B} = \langle B_x, B_y, B_z \rangle\text{,}\)

\begin{equation} \vec{A} \cdot \vec{B} = A_x B_x + A_y B_y + A_z B_z \text{.}\tag{2.7.1} \end{equation}

For two-dimensional vectors, the \(z\) components and the \(A_z B_z\) term of the dot product are zero.
