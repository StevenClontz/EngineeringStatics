Inserting \(dx\ dy\) for \(dA\) and the limits into (10.1.3), and integrating starting with the inside integral gives

\begin{align*} I_x \amp \int_A y^2 dA \\ \amp = \int_0^h \int_0^b y^2\ dx\ dy \\ \amp = \int_0^h y^2 \int_0^b dx \ dy \\ \amp = \int_0^h y^2 \boxed{ b \ dy} \\ \amp = b \int_0^h y^2\ dy \\ \amp = b \left . \frac{y^3}{3} \right \vert_0^h \text{.} \end{align*}
