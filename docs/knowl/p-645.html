The magnitude of a scalar moment is found by by multiplying the magnitude of force \(\vec{F}\) times the moment arm \(d_{\perp}\) as illustrated in FigureÂ 4.2.1. The moment arm is defined as the perpendicular distance from the center of rotation to the line of action of the force, measured perpendicularly.

\begin{equation} M_A ={F} d_{\perp}\text{.}\tag{4.2.1} \end{equation}

To this value you must attach a positive or negative sign to indicate the rotational direction.
