MOI about the \(x\) Axis.

The bottom edge of rectangle 1 is on the \(x\) axis. Using the formula from SubsectionÂ 10.3.2 gives

\begin{equation*} (I_x)_1 = \frac{bh^3}{3} =\frac{ (30)(60)^3}{3} = \mm{2.16 \times 10^6}^4\text{.} \end{equation*}

The centroid of rectangle 2 is located \(\mm{70}\) above the \(x\) axis so we must use the parallel axis theorem (10.3.1), so

\begin{align*} (I_x)_2 \amp = \bar{I} + A d^2\\ \amp = \frac{b h^3}{12} + (b h) d^2\\ \amp = \frac{(90)(20)^3}{12} + (90 \times 20) ( 70)^2\\ (I_x)_2 \amp = \mm{8.88 \times 10^6}^4\text{.} \end{align*}

The moment of inertia of the entire T shape about the \(x\) axis is the sum of these two values,

\begin{equation*} I_x = (I_x)_1 + (I_x)_2 = \mm{11.04 \times 10^6}^4\text{.} \end{equation*}
