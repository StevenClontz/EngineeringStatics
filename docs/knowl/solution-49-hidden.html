For this problem, we need two free-body diagrams. The first links the input force \(F\) to the link force \(BD,\) and the second links \(BD\) to the clamping force \(D_y\text{.}\)

Applying \(\sum M= 0\) at \(A\) to the free-body diagram of the lever gives \(BD\) in terms of \(F\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \text{FBD I:}\quad \Sigma M_A \amp = 0 \\ - BD_x(24) - BD_y(7) - F_x (40) - F_y ( 16) \amp= 0 \\ \left(\frac{7}{25} BD\right) (24) + \left(\frac{24}{25} BD \right) (7) \amp= - (F \cos \ang{60}) ( 40) - (F \sin \ang{60}) (16) \\ 13.44 BD \amp = - 33.86 F\\ BD \amp = - 2.52 F \end{align*}

The negative sign on the answer reveals that although we assumed that \(BD\) acted down and to the left on point \(B\text{,}\) it actually acts up and to the right at an angle of

\begin{equation*} \alpha = \tan^{-1} (24/7) = \ang{73.7} \end{equation*}

from the horizontal. Removing the negative sign give the magnitude of force \vec{BE},

\begin{equation*} BE = 2.52 F\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then \(\sum F_y=0\) on applied to the FBD of the roller will let you solve for \(D_y\) in terms of \(F\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \text{FBD II:}\quad \Sigma F_y \amp = 0 \\ D_y + BD_y \amp= 0 \\ D_y \amp= - \frac{7}{24} BD\\ \amp = - \frac{7}{24} (-2.52 F)\\ \amp = 0.735 F \end{align*}

While you could certainly find \(A_x\text{,}\) \(A_y\) and \(N\) using other equilibrium equations they aren't needed and we don't bother to find them.
