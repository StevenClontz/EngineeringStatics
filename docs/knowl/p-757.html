This chapter will build on what you learned in the previous rigid body equilibrium chapters Chapter 3 and Chapter 5, by helping you to solve multiple-body structures. Broadly defined, a structure is any set of interconnected rigid bodies. The different bodies composing the structure may be able to move relative to one another, such as the blades in a pair of scissors shown below or they may be fixed relative to one another, such as the structural members within a bridge. Static analysis of structures involves the application of the equilibrium equations to solve for the forces acting on and between individual members of the structure.
