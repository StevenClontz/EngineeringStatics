\begin{align*} \bar{x} = \frac{\sum W_i\ \bar{x}_i}{\sum V_i} \amp = \frac{\ft{-88772}^3}{\ft{26633}^2} \amp = \ft{-3.33}\\ \bar{y} = \frac{\sum W_i\ \bar{y}_i}{\sum V_i} \amp = \frac{\ft{69042}^3}{\ft{26633}^2} \amp = \ft{2.59}\\ \bar{z} = \frac{\sum W_i\ \bar{z}_i}{\sum V_i} \amp = \frac{\ft{89717}^3}{\ft{26633}^2} \amp = \ft{3.37} \end{align*}

We have actually found the coordinates of the center of gravity, but since \(g\) is constant they are also coordinates of the center of mass.
