Solution 6.6.17.1.

For this problem, we need two free-body diagrams. The first links the input force \(F\) to the link force \(BD,\) and the second links \(BD\) to the clamping force \(Q\text{.}\)

Since member \(BD\) is a two-force body, the force acting on it is directed along a line-of-action

\begin{equation*} \alpha = \tan^{-1} (24/7) = \ang{73.7} \end{equation*}

from the horizontal, and we assume compression based on the physical situation. Similar triangles gives

\begin{equation*} \sin \alpha =24/25 \text{ and } \cos \alpha = 7/25 \text{.} \end{equation*}

Applying \(\sum M= 0\) at \(A\) to the free-body diagram of the lever gives \(BD\) in terms of \(F\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \text{FBD I:}\quad \Sigma M_A \amp = 0 \\ BD_x(24) + BD_y(7) - F_x (40) - F_y ( 16) \amp= 0 \\ \left(\frac{7}{25} BD\right) (24) + \left(\frac{24}{25} BD \right) (7) \amp= (F \cos \ang{60}) ( 40) + (F \sin \ang{60}) (16) \\ 13.44 BD \amp = 33.86 F\\ BD \amp = 2.52 F \end{align*}

The positive sign on the answer reveals that our assumption that member \(BD\) was in compression was correct.

Applying \(\sum F_y=0\) to the free-body diagram of the roller will give \(Q\) in terms of \(F\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \text{FBD II:}\quad \Sigma F_y \amp = 0 \\ Q - BD_y \amp= 0 \\ Q \amp= \frac{7}{24} BD\\ \amp = \frac{7}{24} (2.52 F)\\ \amp = 0.735 F \end{align*}

While you could certainly find \(A_x\text{,}\) \(A_y\) and \(N\) using other equilibrium equations they weren't asked for and we don't bother to find them.
