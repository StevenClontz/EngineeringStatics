In terrestrial measurements

  1. Coordinate \(r\) is not needed since all points are on the surface of the globe.
  2. Latitude is measured \(\ang{0}\) to \(\ang{180}\) East or West of the prime meridian, rather than \(\ang{0}\) to \(\ang{360}\) counterclockwise from the \(x\) axis.
  3. Longitude is measured \(\ang{0}\) to \(\ang{180}\) North or South of the equator, where as polar angle \(\phi\) is \(\ang{0}\) to \(\ang{180}\) measured from the “North Pole”.
