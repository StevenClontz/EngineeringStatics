Item 3.

The friction resultant can then be assumed to be tangential to a “friction circle.” The friction circle is an imaginary circle of radius \(r_f\) around the center of the shaft and is a function of the radius of the shaft (r) and the friction angle \(\phi_s\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} r_f=r \sin \phi_\text{s} \text{ where, } \phi_\text{s} =\tan^{-1} \mu_\text{s} \end{equation*}
