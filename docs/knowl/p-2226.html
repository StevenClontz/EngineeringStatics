  1. Strategy.

    Determine the properties of the sub shapes with respect to the \(x\) axis, and then use them to find the neutral axis.

    Use the parallel axis theorem to find the moment of inertia of the parts with respect to the neutral axis.

    Take advantage of the fact that the two angles are identical and positioned similarly.

  2. Find the neutral axis.

    For one L\(8\times 4\times 1/2\) angle, from SectionÂ C.1

    \begin{align*} A_\text{L} \amp = \inch{4.75}^2\\ \bar{y}_\text{L} \amp = \inch{1.98}\\ \bar{I}_\text{L} \amp = \inch{17.3}^4\text{.} \end{align*}

    For the \(b = \inch{8},\) \(h = \inch{1}\) rectangle

    \begin{align*} A_\text{R} \amp = bh = \inch{8}^2\\ \bar{y}_\text{R} \amp =-h/2 = \inch{-0.5}\\ \bar{I}_\text{R} \amp = \frac{bh^3}{12} = \frac{8}{12} = \inch{6.67}^4\text{.} \end{align*}

    Find the distance to the neutral axis

    \begin{align*} \bar{y} \amp = \frac{\sum A_i \bar{y_i}}{\sum A_i} = \frac{ 2 A_\text{L} \bar{y}_\text{L} + A_\text{R} \bar{y}_\text{R} } {2 A_\text{L} + A_\text{R}}\\ \amp = \frac{ 2 (4.75)(1.98) + (4)(-0.5)} { 2(4.75) + 4}\\ \bar{y} \amp = \inch{1.245}\text{.} \end{align*}
  3. Find the Moment of Inertia.

    The distance between the neutral axis is and the centroids of the subparts are

    \begin{align*} d_\text{R} \amp = | \bar{y} - \bar{y}_\text{R} | = | 1.245 - (-.0.5)| = \inch{1.745}\\ d_\text{L} \amp = | \bar{y} - \bar{y}_\text{L} | = | 1.245 - 1.98| = \inch{0.735}\text{.} \end{align*}

    The moment of inertia of the rectangle about the \(x'\) axis

    \begin{align*} I_x' \amp = [\bar{I} + A d^2]_\text{R}\\ \amp = 6.67 + (4)(1.745)^2 \\ \amp = \inch{18.85}^4\text{.} \end{align*}

    The moment of inertia of one angle about the \(x'\) axis

    \begin{align*} I_x' \amp = [\bar{I} + A d^2]_\text{R}\\ \amp = 17.3 + (4.75)(0.735)^2 \\ \amp = \inch{19.87}^4\text{.} \end{align*}

    The moment of inertia of the built up beam about the neutral axis

    \begin{align*} I_{x'} \amp = \sum (I_{x'})_i \\ \amp = 2 (I_{x'})_\text{L} + (I_{x'})_\text{R}\\ \amp = 2(19.87) + 18.85\\ I_{x'} \amp = \inch{58.6}^4\text{.} \end{align*}
