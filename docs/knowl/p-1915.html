Finally, the forces along the belt must be in equilibrium to hold the system static. Summing forces along the belt, we find that the tension \(T_1\) plus the distributed friction force \(\Sigma F\) must equal \(T_2\text{.}\) Therefore, \(T_2\) is larger and \(T_1\) is smaller.
\begin{align*} \Sigma F_\text{axial} \amp = 0\\ T_1+\Sigma F -T_2 \amp = 0\\ T_2 \amp = T_1+\Sigma F \end{align*}
