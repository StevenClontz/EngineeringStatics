In English units, the most common unit is the pound-force \(\lbf{1}, \) or pound for short, where one pound is the force which can accelerate a mass of one slug at one foot per second squared. Some textbooks prefer to use pounds mass \(\lbm{}\) instead of slugs, where \(\lbm{1} = \dfrac{\slug{1}}{\aUS{32.2}}\text{.}\)
