Item 4.

In English units, specific weight \(\gamma\) is often used instead of density \(\rho\) to describe fluids. Specific weight is the weight per unit volume of a substance, while density is its mass per unit volume. The two properties are related by

\begin{equation*} \gamma = \rho g\text{.} \end{equation*}

The specific weight of freshwater at room temperature is about \(\pqf{62.4}\text{.}\)
