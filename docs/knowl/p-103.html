Vector directions are described in terms a coordinate system. A coordinate system is an arbitrary reference system used to establish the origin, and the primary directions. You are familiar with the 'Cartesian' coordinate system with mutually perpendicular \(x\text{,}\) \(y\) and \(z\) axes and the origin at their intersection point. Distances are usually measured from the origin, and directions from a primary direction.
