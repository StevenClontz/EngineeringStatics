Solution 4.4.5.a.1.
As you learned in Subsection 4.4.1, Varignon’s Theorem isolates the perpendicular components of \(\vec{r}\) and \(\vec{F}\) and takes adds together the cross products of each perpendicular pair.
As 3:2 does not correspond to a familiar right-triangle ratio, like 3:4:5, the 3:2 slope of \(\vec{F}\) can be changed to an angle \(\theta\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \theta = tan^{-1}\ \frac{3}{2} = \ang{56.3} \end{equation*}
Next, let’s find the components of both \(\vec{r}\) and \(\vec{F}\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} \vec{F} \amp = \lbf{500} \langle cos\ \ang{56.3}, sin\ \ang{56.3} \rangle\\ \amp = \lbf{\langle 277.35, 416.025 \rangle}\\ \vec{r} \amp = \ft{\langle 6,3 \rangle} \end{align*}
Finally, following Varignon’s Theorem, we’ll cross just the perpendicular component pairs.
\begin{align*} \vec{M_A} \amp = \vec{r} \times \vec{F}\\ \amp = \left(\vec{r_x} \times \vec{F_y}\right) + \left(\vec{r_y} \times \vec{F_x}\right)\\ \amp = \ft{6}\left(\lbf{416.025}\right)\left(\khat\right)+\ft{3}\left(\lbf{277.35}\right)\left(-\khat\right)\\ \amp = \ftlbf{1664.1}\left(+\khat\right) \end{align*}
Notes:
  • For two-dimensional vectors in component form, this is often the preferred method for computing moments as it is quick and most find the process intuitive.
  • The first cross product, \(\left(\vec{r_x} \times \vec{F_y}\right) \text{,}\) has a postitve value because \(\ihat\times\jhat=+\khat\text{,}\) not because you are simply multiplying two postive components.
  • The second cross product, \(\left(\vec{r_y} \times \vec{F_x}\right)\text{,}\) results in a negative value because \(\jhat\times\ihat=-\khat\text{.}\) You can confirm this with either the three-finger or slide-and-curl right-hand rule methods you learned in Subsection 2.8.1.
  • The units of \(\vec{M_A}\) are a force times a distance \(ft \cdot lbf\text{.}\) All moments will have similar units, force times distance.
  • Given that we used the right-hand rule to determine the sign of each moment term, the overall sign of \(\vec{M_A}\) determines the final direction. Where
    • a positive answer corresponds to a CCW moment - right thumb out of the page with the moment in your fingertip direction (for hand diagram, see Figure 4.1.2), and
    • a negative answer would indicate a CW moment - right thumb into the page with the moment in your fingertip direction.
