Solution 4.7.5.1.

First, replace the couple with an equivalent couple, \(M_3\text{,}\) the magnitude of which is

\begin{align*} M_3 \amp = F d_\perp\\ \amp = F d \sin \ang{60}\\ \amp = \Nm{69.2} \end{align*}

By observation, this is a counterclockwise moment as is \(M_2\text{.}\) \(M_1\) is clockwise. Summing the scalar magnitudes gives the resultant moment. The signs of the terms are assigned according to the sign convention: positive if counterclockwise, negative if clockwise.

\begin{align*} M_R \amp = \Sigma M\\ \amp = M_1 + M_2 + M_3\\ \amp = - \Nm{400} + \Nm{200} + \Nm{69.2}\\ \amp = -\Nm{130.7}\\ \amp = \Nm{130.7} \text{ clockwise} \end{align*}
