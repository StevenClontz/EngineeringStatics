Solution 7.8.3.1.

The equivalent load is the ‘area’ under the rectangular load intensity curve and it acts straight down at the centroid. This loading has a \(\ft{6}\) base and a \(\lbperft{10}\) height so

\begin{equation*} W = b h = (\ft{6}) (\lbperft{10}) =\lb{60}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The centroid is located in the middle of the rectangle so,

\begin{equation*} \bar{x} = \ft{4}\text{.} \end{equation*}
