Example 4.3.4. Slider.

In this example we look at the conditions of equilibrium by considering the load and the constraints, rather than taking a global equilibrium approach which considers both the load and reaction forces.

The interactive diagram for this problem will help you visualize the relationship between angle \(\alpha\) and the resultant load force \(R\text{.}\) When in equilibrium, the rod will supply a reaction force equal and opposite to the resultant.

(a)

Three forces act on a machine part which is free to slide along a vertical, frictionless rod. Forces \(A\) and \(B\) have a magnitude of \(\N{20}\) and force \(C\) has a magnitude of \(\N{30}\text{.}\) Force \(B\) acts \(\alpha\) degrees from the horizontal, and force \(C\) acts at the same angle from the vertical.

Determine the angle \(\alpha\) required for equilibrium, and the magnitude and direction of the reaction force acting on the slider.
