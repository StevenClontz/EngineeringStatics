To emphasize that unit vectors are pure direction, track what happens when a vector is divided by its magnitude
\begin{equation*} \textrm{unit vector} = \frac{\vec{F}}{| \vec{F} | } = \frac{\textrm{[vector]}}{\textrm{[magnitude]}}=\frac{\cancel{\textrm{[magnitude]}} \cdot\textrm{[direction]}}{\cancel{\textrm{[magnitude]}}} = \textrm{[direction]} \text{.} \end{equation*}
