Notice that the moment and the angular acceleration are vector quantities and thus have both a magnitude and direction. The mass moment of inertia, on the other hand, is a scalar quantity and has only a magnitude. Vectors and scalars will be covered in more detail in ChapterÂ 3. As all the bodies in Statics are subject to a balance of moments \(\Sigma \vec{M}=0\text{,}\) in addition to a balance of forces, it turns out that the angular acceleration \(\vec{\alpha}\) also must be equal to zero. Therefore all bodies in equilibrium are either at rest or remain at a constant angular velocity.
