Newton's first law also applies to moments and rotational velocities. A moment (or torque) is the rotational tendency of a force and you will learn much more about moments in ChapterÂ 4. A body will maintain its current rotational velocity until a moment (applied torque) is exerted to change that rotational velocity. This can be seen in things like toy tops, flywheels, stationary bikes, and other objects that will continue spinning once started until brakes or a moment either speeds up or slows down the spinning motion.
