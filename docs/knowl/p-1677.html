Shear and bending moment digrams show the effect of the load on the internal forces within the beam and are a graphical representation of equations (8.6.1)–(8.6.4). The diagrams are made up of jumps, slopes and areas as a result of the load.

  • Jumps are vertical changes in shear and moment diagrams.

  • Slopes are gradual changes in shear and moment diagrams. Positive slopes go up and to the right.

  • Areas are “areas” under the loading and shear curves. The area under the loading curve is actually a force, and the area under the shear curve is actually a moment.
