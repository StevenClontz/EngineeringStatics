To find the resultant vector \(\vec{R}\) of two one-dimensional vectors \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) you can use the tip-to-tail technique in FigureÂ 2.1.1 below. In the tip-to-tail technique, you slide vector \(\vec{B}\) until its tail is at the tip of \(\vec{A}\text{,}\) and the vector from the tail of \(\vec{A}\) to the tip of \(\vec{B}\) is the resultant \(\vec{R}\text{.}\) Note that the resultant \(\vec{R}\) is the same when you add \(\vec{A}\) onto \(\vec{B}\text{,}\) so the order of vector addition does not matter and is considered commutative.
