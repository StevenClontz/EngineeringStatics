As their name implies, zero-force members are not engaged by the specific loads applied to a truss. While you can find zero-force members using either Method of Joints or Sections, you can save a lot of computational time by identifying them with two simple rules.

  • Rule 1: If two members meet at an unloaded joint, then both members are zero-force members.
  • Rule 2: If three forces (interaction, reaction, or applied forces) meet at a joint and two are collinear, then the third is a zero-force member.
