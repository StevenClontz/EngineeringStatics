Varignon’s Theorem is a method of calculating moments developed in 1687 by French mathematician Pierre Varignon (1654 – 1722). It states that the sum of the moments of several concurrent forces is equal to the moment of the resultant of those forces. In other words, to find the moment of a series of concurrent forces about a given point, we can add the forces to find the resultant and then calculate the moment of that resultant about the point of interest, or compute the moment of each individual force about the point of interest and then sum those moments to find the net moment about that point. Either method will give us equivalent results.
