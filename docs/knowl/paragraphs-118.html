Strategy.

First, divide the area into four parts:

  1. a \(\inch{9} \times \inch{3}\) rectangle
  2. a \(\inch{6} \times \inch{3}\) rectangle
  3. a \(\inch{3} \times \inch{4}\) rectangle, and
  4. a removed quarter-circle with a \(\inch{3}\) radius.

Then set up a table and apply the parallel axis theorem (10.3.1) as in the previous example. Since the quarter-circle is removed, subtract its moment of inertia from total of the other shapes.
