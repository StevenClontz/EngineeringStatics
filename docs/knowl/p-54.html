As a typical example across the sections below, consider that two cables are pulling on an eye bolt with forces of \(\vec{A} = 5~\text{ lb} \)\(60°\) above the \(x\) axis, and \(\vec{B} = 3~\text{ lb} \) acting at a 45° angle below the x axis. The tension of a cable is the magnitude of the force that the cable applies, and is a scalar.
