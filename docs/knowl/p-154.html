One common way to define the direction of a three-dimensional vector is by using direction cosine angles, also commonly known as coordinate direction angles. The direction cosine angles are the three angle between the positive \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) and \(z\) axes to a given vector and are called \(\theta_x\text{,}\) \(\theta_y\text{,}\) and \(\theta_z\text{,}\) respectively. Three dimensional vectors, components, and angle are difficult to visualize.
