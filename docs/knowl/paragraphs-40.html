Examine the roller at \(D\).

The roller is a three-force body, so the lines of action of \(N\text{,}\) \(D_y\text{,}\) and \(BD\) are coincident and it may be treated as a particle.

Equilibrium analysis shows that \(N\) and \(D_y\) must oppose the horizontal and vertical components of force \(BD\text{.}\) We have assumed that member \(BD\) is in tension, but it is actually in compression so vector \(BD\) points in the opposite direction of the force shown on the free-body diagram. This will be revealed by the analysis.

The clamping force produced by the toggle clamp appears on this free-body diagram so it will be important later for the solution.

Tips.

  • Recognize three-force bodies and use their special properties to your advantage.

  • Use the same name for the exposed forces on interacting bodies since they are equal-and-opposite halves of an action-reaction pair.
