When the impending motion of the screw is in the direction of the applied force, we can also state that the “applied force supports impending motion.” This case occurs any time you remove a force held by a screw (like loosening a screw clamp). This motion case is a bit more complicated than the force opposing impending motion, as there are three different cases determined by the relative magnitude of the friction angle \(\phi_\text{s}\) and the thread angle \(\alpha\text{.}\) The basic derivation and variables in the case-specific equations below are quite similar to the derivation for (9.2), but use subtly different FBDs (see Figure 9.20 below).
