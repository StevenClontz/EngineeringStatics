Stable vs. Unstable.

A body in equilibrium is held in position by its supports, which restrict the bodyâ€™s motion and counteract the applied loads. When there are sufficient supports to restrain a body from moving, we say that the body is stable. A stable body is prevented from translating and rotating in all directions. A body which can move is unstable even if it is not currently moving, because the slightest change in load may take it out of equilibrium and initiate motion. Stability is loading independent i.e. a stable body is stable for any loading condition.
